Top Five Things You Won’t Want to Miss at 404 The countdown is on, less than a week away until the very first 404.

29 SEP 2017
The countdown is on, less than a week away until the very first 404. We can’t wait to bring together like-minded people, create ideas and celebrate all that’s awesome in tech right now with speakers, installations, demos and live performances. Here are the top five things you won’t want to miss at 404. Have you got your ticket?

 

1. Discover the creative uses of artificial intelligence from the 1980s to today

You might have heard about or even experimented with today's tools for creative AI. You can develop paintings, write poetry (depending on your definition), and even make songs using deep learning networks. But did you know one of the first AI and art collaborations began in the late 80s? Katherine Jarmul will take us on a stroll through the history of artificial intelligence and its artistic creations ending with some fun experiments you can run on your computer!

Katharine Jarmul is a pythonista and founder of Kjamistan, a data consulting company in Berlin. She’s been using Python since 2008 to solve and create problems

2. Take part in an innovative experiment that blends live music performance and gaming.

Cello Fortress is a unique combination of a game and a live music performance. A cellist defends a fortress by improvising on his cello. Melodies control the guns, dissonant notes activate the flamethrowers. Players from the audience use game controllers to steer their tanks and attack the fortress.

The cellist plays live music, while at the same time controlling the game to be a fun challenge for the players.

Cellist Joost van Dongen is known for his previous game Proun, and as lead programmer and co-founder of Ronimo Games, the studio behind the hit indie games Awesomenauts, Swords & Soldiers and De Blob. Joost regularly writes about creating games on his development blog.

 

3. Dance the night away to Javascript DJs

At our exclusive after-party, dance the night away to { Live : JS } with Ruth John and Sam Wray. { Live : JS } is a collective of audio and visual artists who build live experiences, shows and installations. Why JS? Because that's the main technology they use: JavaScript.

Ruth is a web technologist and Google Developer Expert. She likes to educate people about new web technologies and inspire them to try them, coming up with exciting and engaging ways to use them. She’s a founding member of { Live: JS }, and her favourite things include interactive audio/visual installations and 80s cartoons, usually at the same time!

Sam is a musician and digital artist. He is a creator of visuals with his software modV and a writer of music using Nintendo Game Boys under the alias 2xAA.

4. Get struck by one of our many lightning talks!

Throughout the day at 404, we’ll have a whole host of 2-minute lightning talks by local tech community user group organisers. Featuring community groups such as Coding Grace, Dublin Scala, 3DCamp Dublin, WITS Ireland, Machine Learning Dublin, Dublin UX, Dublin C/C++ User Group, Women in Tech Africa and countless others, you won’t want to miss these! Check out the line-up here.

5. The opportunity to network with some of the top tech companies in Ireland

What do Accenture Dock, CarTrawler, Aer Lingus, and SIG all have in common? They're all going to be at 404 to network with experienced techies! Also attending will be Ward, Liferay, Techies 4 Temple Street, Bearing One, Nitro, Pramerica and Pivotal.

Discuss their latest innovations and opportunities and find out how you can collaborate with them while checking out our line-up of demos, installations and performances, all in the unique surroundings of the Royal Hospital Kilmainham.  

You won’t want to miss out. Book your ticket now!
