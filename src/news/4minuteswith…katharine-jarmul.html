#4MinutesWith…Katharine Jarmul 

2 OCT 2017
As part of our #4MinutesWith series, we spoke to 404 speaker and founder of Kjamistan, Katharine Jarmul, about her career to date, what makes a special tech event and what she’s looking forward to at 404.

Briefly outline your profession?

Data Science is a bit hard to outline briefly as it's come to represent a lot of different roles and responsibilities in most organizations. A data scientist may implement recommendation algorithms or design models for interpreting text. She may also scale data ingestion and manage large engineering workflows to process data. He may work on graphs and dashboards in business intelligence software. And there are many other possibilities depending on the needs of the organization and where the data-related priorities lie. It is a bit of an expansive field!

 

How did you get started out in this field?

Like many, by accident! I started working with data and code as a journalist at the Washington Post in 2008; where I also first learned Python. I became very interested in story telling with data, and continued to pursue this after leaving journalism. After several jobs as lead engineer and management, I formed my own company, Kjamistan, which I now run as a data consultant, speaker and trainer.

 

What will you be talking about at 404?

Algorithmic Art: its history and origins to present day works. I will also present artistic takes on machine learning and artificial intelligence. Finally, I demonstrate a few ways to start building your own algorithmic art. I am a big fan of some of the artists I will be presenting about, so I'm happy to spread the passion with the attendees and organizers! 

 

What’s your proudest achievement to date?

Probably being part of the initial chapter (and spread) of PyLadies, a group dedicated to spreading diversity in open source (specifically Python). It was a great time and a lot of work, and now there are PyLadies chapters all over the world! 

 

What do you hope to achieve next?

German fluency, publishing my first peer-reviewed paper and catching up on my long list of papers to read! 

 

What’s the biggest challenge facing the tech sector?

The issues of fairness, discrimination and the adjoining issue of data privacy. In a "big data" landscape, there are a lot of ways to build unethical models and create or perpetuate unfair treatment. There are also many ways to violate privacy (intentionally or unintentionally). These are things I worry about and therefore, I often engage in dialogue (or keep up on research) with my peers regarding these topics. 

 

What makes a really special tech event?

Diversity! And great minds! I love when I meet folks passionate about their field of study or their work; it is really inspiring and I often learn something new. 

 

What are you most looking forward to at 404?  

You mean other than enjoying the lovely city of Dublin, having a pint and some fish & chips and meeting a bunch of great folks? And I don't even have to leave the EU! Probably the installations -- especially the Cello Fortress with Joost van Dongen. Sounds amazing!
